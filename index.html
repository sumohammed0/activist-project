Minimizing food insecurity and homelessness locally one step at a time

A GROWING PROBLEM

Dallas has the largest homeless population in Texas, about 26,000 people. This is a result of rising prices in the housing market as well as an increase in the cost of living. Alongside homelessness, people are not able to afford food and struggle to eat enough for an active, healthy life.

STARTING FROM THE BOTTOM

Food security varies household to houshold. There are four levels of food security : high security, marginal security, low security, and very low security. We aim to help by taking it from the very bottom and helping people at the lowest level of food insecurity, the homeless.
